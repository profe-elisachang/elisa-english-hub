Once owned by the late legendary French-Italian fashion designer Pierre Cardin, the Bubble Palace is a remarkable structure on France's Mediterranean coast near Cannes. The massive estate boasts 29 rooms, including 10 bedrooms and 11 bathrooms. It also features multiple swimming pools and 8,500 square meters of tropical gardens. Additionally, the complex houses an open-air theater with seating for 500 people and a stunning view of the Mediterranean Sea.

What makes the pink-hued structure so striking, though, is its radical, futuristic architecture. It is made up of a series of connected bubble-like structures that evoke a hybrid between prehistoric caves and something out of a science fiction movie. Hungarian architect Antti Lovag, who came up with the idea for the unique design of the Bubble Palace, said he envisioned a living space inspired by the natural shelters that early humans once inhabited. Lovag deliberately avoided the use of straight lines, which he considered "an aggression against nature," and adopted curves instead of corners.
