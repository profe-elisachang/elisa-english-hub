Power banks offer a convenient way to charge electronic devices such as smartphones and tablets. Although these portable chargers can prevent small gadgets from running out of power, they also carry a serious potential risk: fire or explosion.

While such terrifying incidents are relatively rare, the risk cannot be dismissed. Power banks contain lithium-ion batteries and other combustible materials, which increase their potential to be a hazard. Lithium-ion cells are energy-dense and use electrolytes that are flammable under certain conditions. Overheating or internal damage can trigger a chain reaction called thermal runaway, during which the battery's temperature rises uncontrollably. This may cause the battery to swell, emit smoke, burst into flames, or even explode, and the process cannot stop on its own. Documented cases have occurred while users were asleep and even during flights.

Beyond the internal risks of lithium-ion batteries, several external factors can also increase the likelihood of a fire. The most critical is poor manufacturing quality. Low-cost models are more likely to have inferior components and lack proper safety features, whereas higher-quality devices include protective mechanisms against overheating. Overcharging is another major contributor, particularly when the charger is left unattended for extended periods, which can lead to heat buildup. Dropping power banks may also pose a risk of explosion.
