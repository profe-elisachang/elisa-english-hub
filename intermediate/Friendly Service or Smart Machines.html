üìÑ Article: Friendly Service or Smart Machines?

Dining out can take on very different forms, depending on whether you step into a restaurant that follows tradition or one that embraces technology. The left picture presents a classic restaurant setting: a human waiter jotting down an order while interacting with a customer face to face. This service style highlights personal connection and attentive care, with small talk or a warm smile often making the dining experience more enjoyable. By contrast, the right picture paints a more futuristic setting, where a robot waiter delivers dishes to the table with precision. Here, technology takes center stage by demonstrating efficiency and novelty.

I would have human waiters greet customers, lead them to their tables, and take orders, since they can offer personalized recommendations and make people feel welcome. Meanwhile, robot waiters could handle repetitive tasks such as carrying heavy trays or cleaning tables.
