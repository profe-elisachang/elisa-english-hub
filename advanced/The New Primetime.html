Answer 1: Summary

The article explains that Gen Z and millennials are shifting away from traditional television and movies toward social media creators, who they find more relevant and relatable. This "new primetime" represents a fundamental change in entertainment, where influencers have replaced TV personalities as the preferred source of content. However, the shift isn't entirely one-way‚Äîsome creators are moving to traditional media for more stable income, while celebrities are establishing social media presence to build their brands.

Answer 2: Vocabulary Application (Examples)

  • have the upper hand: "In safety negotiations, the EHS team has the upper hand when they present accident statistics and regulatory requirements."
  • niche: "Our IT department specializes in the niche area of industrial automation systems for semiconductor manufacturing."
  • juggling: "Process engineers are constantly juggling quality control, production deadlines, and equipment maintenance schedules."
  • lucrative: "Investing in employee safety training can be lucrative in the long term by reducing accident costs and insurance premiums."
  • worth the hassle: "Implementing the new ERP system is definitely worth the hassle if it streamlines our production reporting."

Answer 3: Personal Reflection

Content creators moving to traditional media gain advantages like stable contracts, guaranteed income, and access to larger production budgets. However, they may lose creative freedom, direct audience connection, and the ability to respond quickly to trends. Similarly, in engineering careers, moving from startup environments to large corporations offers stability and resources but may reduce innovation opportunities and flexibility. The "grass is greener" depends on individual priorities‚Äîsome value security while others prioritize autonomy.

Answer 4: Critical Thinking

Social media creators build connection through authenticity, frequent interaction, direct communication (comments, live streams), and consistent content that audiences can relate to personally. Companies could adopt similar strategies by: encouraging managers to communicate regularly and transparently, creating spaces for employee feedback and dialogue, sharing behind-the-scenes content about company decisions, and ensuring leadership is accessible and responsive. In Taiwan-based companies operating in Mexico, this might mean using WeChat, LINE, or Teams to maintain regular touchpoints between management and local teams.
